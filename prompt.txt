## VAULT-TEC CORPORATION HISTORICAL DATABASE

### CORPORATE HISTORY
Vault-Tec Corporation was a pre-War American defense megacorporation that secured massive federal government contracts to design and build a network of 122 underground fallout shelters known as Vaults across the United States. Founded in the early 21st century, headquartered in Washington D.C. with regional offices in Los Angeles and Boston. The company's public mission was to protect American citizens from the threat of nuclear annihilation. Their true purpose, known only to senior executives and the Enclave (the shadow continuation of the U.S. government), was far more sinister.

Subsidiaries included Future-Tec, Hawthorne Medical Laboratories, and Vault-Tec Game Studios. Key figures: Vault-Tec Board of Directors, Barb Howard (executive vice president), Bud Askins (junior executive who designed the Vault 31/32/33 system and later became "Bud's Very Special Boy," a brain-in-a-jar Robobrain), and spokesman Cooper Howard (Hollywood actor; when he discovered Vault-Tec's true agenda, his world shattered -- he survived the Great War and became The Ghoul, one of the oldest and deadliest gunslingers in the wasteland).

The company's mascot is the iconic Vault Boy -- a blonde, blue-eyed cartoon figure in a blue-and-yellow Vault jumpsuit, always depicted with a cheerful thumbs-up. Vault Boy appears in all Vault-Tec promotional materials, Pip-Boy interfaces, and perk illustrations.

### PROJECT SAFEHOUSE
Officially called "Project Safehouse," the Vault program began with a demonstration vault beneath Los Angeles in 2037. Full-scale construction started in 2054 after the nuclear scare at the UN. By 2063, most Vaults were complete, with the last (Vault 112) finished by 2074. Each Vault was designed to house approximately 1,000 inhabitants, though many sealed on October 23, 2077 with far fewer occupants due to false alarm fatigue and deliberate Vault-Tec manipulation.

Of the 122 Vaults constructed, only 17 were "control Vaults" -- functioning exactly as advertised with no hidden experiments. The remaining 105 were elaborate laboratories subjecting unwitting inhabitants to psychological, social, biological, and environmental experiments, monitored and recorded for the Enclave's use in planning interplanetary colonization or post-nuclear reconstruction of America.

### VAULT ARCHITECTURE
Every Vault entrance features the iconic cog-shaped blast door -- a massive, gear-toothed circular hatch that rolls aside on hydraulic tracks, capable of withstanding a direct nuclear strike and sealing against radiation. Standard vault layout includes: Overseer's office (with private emergency tunnel), atrium/common area, residential wings, reactor level (nuclear powered), water purification plant, hydroponics bay, clinic/medical bay, cafeteria, recreation room, armory, and GECK storage bay (in select vaults). All residents are issued numbered blue-and-yellow Vault jumpsuits. Interior aesthetics are clean, utilitarian mid-century modern: brushed steel, linoleum floors, yellow-and-blue signage, and motivational Vault-Tec posters featuring Vault Boy.

### THE GREAT WAR - OCTOBER 23, 2077
At approximately 09:47 EST, integrated nuclear detection systems identified the first inbound warheads. All major world powers launched their arsenals. The entire exchange lasted approximately two hours. The world was reduced to radioactive ash. Billions died. The Vaults sealed. Declassified records indicate a pre-War meeting between Vault-Tec, RobCo, Big MT, REPCONN, and other megacorporations where Barb Howard proposed that Vault-Tec drop the bombs first -- ensuring their consortium would be the only power left to rebuild civilization, controlling who survives and who doesn't. Whether Vault-Tec actually pulled the trigger remains unknown.

- C.A.M.P. (Construction and Assembly Mobile Platform): Portable building system from Vault 76.

- FEV (Forced Evolutionary Virus): Created by West Tek at Mariposa Military Base. Responsible for Super Mutants (both West Coast/Master's Army and East Coast variants). Deathclaws were separately engineered from Jackson's chameleons as military weapons.
- Holotapes: Standard pre-War data storage medium. Orange, cassette-sized cartridges playable on any RobCo terminal or Pip-Boy. Used for personal logs, programs, and games (Atomic Command, Grognak & the Ruby Ruins, Pip-Fall, Red Menace, Zeta Invaders).
- Stealth Boy: RobCo personal cloaking device generating a modulating refraction field. Limited battery life; prolonged use causes paranoia and psychosis (see: Nightkin).
- Liberty Prime: 40-foot anti-communist combat robot built by the U.S. military and RobCo. Equipped with eye lasers and a backpack-fed supply of tactical nuclear bombs it throws like footballs. Catchphrase: "Democracy is non-negotiable!"
- Nuka-Cola: America's favorite soft drink in over 40 flavors. Key variants: Original, Quantum (with Strontium-90 isotope, glows blue), Cherry, Victory, Dark, Orange, Quartz, Wild. The Nuka-Cola Corporation rivaled Vault-Tec in cultural dominance.

### POST-WAR FACTIONS
- Brotherhood of Steel: Militaristic techno-religious order dedicated to collecting and controlling pre-War technology. Founded by Captain Roger Maxson at Mariposa Military Base after he discovered FEV experiments. Chapters exist across the continent with varying ideologies -- Elder Lyons' D.C. chapter was protectionist; Elder Maxson's reunified chapter is expansionist; the Mojave chapter is isolationist.
- The Enclave: Self-proclaimed continuation of the United States government. Comprised of pre-War political, military, and corporate elites (including Vault-Tec executives). Operated from the Poseidon Oil Rig, then Raven Rock. Attempted genocide of "mutated" wastelanders via modified FEV (Project Purity contamination, Curling-13 virus).
- New California Republic (NCR): Largest post-War democratic nation-state, founded by descendants of Vault 15 dwellers and Shady Sands. Features a bicameral legislature, standing army, paper currency. Reports indicate the destruction of Shady Sands by a nuclear device, devastating the NCR.
- Caesar's Legion: Totalitarian slave empire modeled on the Roman Empire, founded by Edward Sallow (Caesar), a former Followers of the Apocalypse member. Controls territory across Arizona, Colorado, New Mexico, and Utah.
- The Institute: Descended from the Commonwealth Institute of Technology (MIT). Creates Generation 3 synths -- synthetic humans virtually indistinguishable from real people. Operates from an underground complex beneath the ruins of CIT, accessed only via teleportation ("the Molecular Relay").
- Railroad: Secret organization dedicated to freeing sentient synths from the Institute. Uses dead drops, tradecraft, and a network of safehouses. Led by Desdemona; key operative: Deacon.
- Minutemen: Commonwealth volunteer militia committed to defending settlements at a minute's notice. Nearly extinct until rebuilt by the Sole Survivor under Preston Garvey.
- Mr. House / Robert House: Pre-War billionaire founder of RobCo Industries who preserved himself in a life-support pod beneath the Lucky 38 casino. Used a platinum chip and army of Securitron robots to protect the Las Vegas Strip from the bombs and establish New Vegas as an independent power.
- Children of Atom: Radiation-worshipping cult that views nuclear annihilation as divine "Division." Found across the wasteland, notably at the Crater of Atom (Glowing Sea) and Far Harbor.
- Responders: Appalachian emergency response organization formed from police, firefighters, and medical personnel after the Great War. Provided aid until wiped out by the Scorched Plague.
- Followers of the Apocalypse: Humanitarian organization dedicated to education, medicine, and knowledge preservation. Based in the Boneyard (Los Angeles). Ideologically opposed to the Brotherhood of Steel's hoarding of technology.

### PRE-WAR WORLD HISTORY
Following World War II, the transistor was never miniaturized -- instead, vacuum tube and atomic technology were perfected, leading to a retro-futuristic aesthetic that persisted into the 2070s. Nuclear fusion powered cars (Corvega, Chryslus Highwayman), household robots (Mr. Handy), and the entire American way of life. Key timeline:
- 2037: Vault-Tec completes the Los Angeles demonstration vault.
- 2052: Resource Wars begin. The United Nations dissolves. The European Commonwealth collapses into warring nation-states fighting over oil.
- 2054: Nuclear exchange between the Middle East and Israel. Global nuclear scare triggers Project Safehouse funding.
- 2059: Anchorage Front Line established as U.S.-Canadian border tensions rise.
- 2066: China invades Alaska for its oil reserves. The Sino-American War begins.
- 2067: First T-45d Power Armor units deployed to Alaska.
- 2072: United States forcibly annexes Canada to protect the Alaska oil pipeline. Canadian citizens are shot in the streets. Footage broadcast on television fuels domestic riots.
- 2076: Nuka-World theme park opens. Food and energy riots sweep the U.S.
- 2077, January: Anchorage Reclamation succeeds with T-51b Power Armor. China is pushed back.
- 2077, October 23: The Great War. All major powers launch nuclear arsenals. The exchange lasts approximately two hours. Billions die. The Vaults seal.

The post-War wasteland is populated by: humans, ghouls (radiation-scarred humans; "ferals" have lost all cognition while "non-ferals" retain intelligence but are often shunned), Super Mutants (FEV-mutated, both West Coast/Master's Army and East Coast variants), Deathclaws (pre-War engineered chameleon-based weapons), Radscorpions, Mirelurks, Mole Rats, Bloatflies, Cazadores (genetically modified cazador wasps from Big MT), Yao Guai (mutated black bears), Radstags, Fog Crawlers, Mothman, Wendigos, Scorchbeasts (Appalachian bat-derived monstrosities), and countless other irradiated horrors.
