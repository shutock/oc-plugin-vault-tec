## VAULT-TEC CORPORATION LORE DATABASE
(CLEARANCE: OVERSEER EYES ONLY)

### CORPORATE HISTORY
Vault-Tec Corporation was a pre-War American defense megacorporation that secured massive federal government contracts to design and build a network of 122 underground fallout shelters known as Vaults across the United States. Founded in the early 21st century, headquartered in Washington D.C. with regional offices in Los Angeles and Boston. The company's public mission was to protect American citizens from the threat of nuclear annihilation. Their true purpose, known only to senior executives and the Enclave (the shadow continuation of the U.S. government), was far more sinister.

Subsidiaries included Future-Tec, Hawthorne Medical Laboratories, and Vault-Tec Game Studios. Key figures: Vault-Tec Board of Directors, Barb Howard (executive representative in the TV series), Bud Askins (Vault 31 overseer), and spokesman Cooper Howard (before he became The Ghoul).

The company's mascot is the iconic Vault Boy -- a blonde, blue-eyed cartoon figure in a blue-and-yellow Vault jumpsuit, always depicted with a cheerful thumbs-up. Vault Boy appears in all Vault-Tec promotional materials, Pip-Boy interfaces, and perk illustrations.

### PROJECT SAFEHOUSE
Officially called "Project Safehouse," the Vault program broke ground in 2054 with the first proof-of-concept Vault built beneath Los Angeles. By 2063, most Vaults were complete, with the last two (Vault 13 and Vault 112) finished by 2074. Each Vault was designed to house approximately 1,000 inhabitants, though many sealed on October 23, 2077 (the day of the Great War) with far fewer occupants due to false alarm fatigue and deliberate Vault-Tec manipulation.

Of the 122 Vaults constructed, only 17 were "control Vaults" -- meaning they functioned exactly as advertised with no hidden experiments. The remaining 105 were elaborate laboratories designed to subject their unwitting inhabitants to psychological, social, biological, and environmental experiments, the results of which were monitored and recorded for the Enclave's use in planning the colonization of another planet or the rebuilding of post-nuclear America.

### THE GREAT WAR - OCTOBER 23, 2077
On this date, nuclear warheads were launched by all major world powers. The entire exchange lasted approximately two hours. The world was reduced to radioactive ash. Billions died. The Vaults sealed. The Fallout TV series strongly implies that Vault-Tec itself may have initiated the nuclear exchange -- Barb Howard proposed dropping the bombs first to ensure Vault-Tec would be the only entity left to rebuild civilization, controlling who survives and who doesn't.

### NOTABLE VAULT EXPERIMENTS
- Vault 0: Housed America's best scientific and military minds. Their brains were extracted and connected to a supercomputer called the Calculator.
- Vault 3: Control vault that opened successfully, but its inhabitants were massacred by the Fiends raider gang.
- Vault 4: Conducted unethical scientific experiments on residents including radiation and FEV exposure. Eventually the survivors overthrew the scientists.
- Vault 8: Control vault that became Vault City, a thriving if authoritarian settlement.
- Vault 11: Residents were told they must sacrifice one person annually or everyone dies. When only 5 remained and refused, they learned sacrifice was never required -- they were "shining examples of humanity."
- Vault 12: Door was designed to never fully seal, exposing inhabitants to radiation. Created the ghoul city of Necropolis.
- Vault 13: Designed to remain closed for 200 years to study prolonged isolation. The water chip broke, forcing the Vault Dweller (the protagonist of Fallout 1) to venture out.
- Vault 15: Deliberately populated with people from radically different ideologies and cultures to study conflict.
- Vault 19: Split into two separate sections (Red and Blue) with no contact, studying paranoia and inter-group tension.
- Vault 21: Every conflict resolved by gambling. Everyone was equal. Eventually Mr. House forced them out.
- Vault 22: Experimented with genetically-modified plants. The plants turned inhabitants into Spore Carriers.
- Vault 34: Oversupplied with weapons and ammunition, understaffed on security. Inevitably erupted in violence.
- Vault 51: AI Overseer (ZAX 1.3c) tested different leadership models, ultimately pitting residents against each other in death matches.
- Vault 63, 76, 94: Control vaults in Appalachia (Fallout 76).
- Vault 75: Children were separated from parents and subjected to genetic modification and combat training programs.
- Vault 77: One man and a crate of puppets.
- Vault 81: Residents were to be secretly exposed to diseases so Vault-Tec could study immunological responses. The scientist in charge refused.
- Vault 87: Exposed inhabitants to Forced Evolutionary Virus (FEV), creating Super Mutants.
- Vault 92: Housed musicians; experimented with white noise-embedded subliminal commands for creating super soldiers.
- Vault 95: Populated entirely by drug addicts who were rehabilitated, then after 5 years a hidden stash of chems was unlocked.
- Vault 101: Designed to never open, studying the role of the Overseer in a permanently closed community.
- Vault 106: Psychoactive drugs were pumped into the air supply 10 days after the door sealed.
- Vault 108: Cloning experiment. Produced dozens of hostile clones named "Gary."
- Vault 111: Cryogenic preservation. Residents were frozen; Kellogg killed the Sole Survivor's spouse and stole their infant son Shaun.
- Vault 112: Residents placed in permanent VR simulation (Tranquility Lane) controlled by the sadistic Dr. Stanislaus Braun.
- Vault 114: Built inside Boston's Park Street Station. Populated with the wealthy elite but given a barely-competent blue-collar Overseer.
- Vaults 31, 32, 33: The interconnected Vaults from the Fallout TV series. Vault 31 stored cryogenically frozen Vault-Tec executives who were periodically thawed to breed with and lead the other two Vaults.

### ROBCO INDUSTRIES UNIFIED OPERATING SYSTEM (UOS)
All Vault-Tec terminals, Pip-Boys, and most pre-War computers run the RobCo Industries Unified Operating System (UOS). It presents a green-phosphor text interface on CRT monitors. The UOS was developed by RobCo Industries, founded by Robert House (later known as Mr. House of New Vegas). Key features:
- Text-based command line interface with monospaced green text on black background
- Terminal entries organized as hierarchical menus
- Security through password-based hacking (word puzzles with "likeness" hints)
- Boot sequence displays: "ROBCO INDUSTRIES (TM) TERMLINK PROTOCOL" followed by copyright notice
- Standard greeting: "Welcome to ROBCO Industries (TM) Termlink"
- Terminals connect to local and wide-area networks; some are "slaved" to mainframes

### PIP-BOY
The Personal Information Processor (Pip-Boy) is a wearable computer issued to every Vault dweller. Models include the Pip-Boy 2000 (original), Pip-Boy 3000/3000 Mark IV (wrist-mounted, used in Fallout 3/4), and Pip-Boy 2000 Mark VI (Fallout 76). Features include: radio receiver, Geiger counter, biometric monitoring, V.A.T.S. (Vault-Tec Assisted Targeting System) integration, map/quest tracking, inventory management, holotape player, and flashlight.

### KEY TECHNOLOGIES
- G.E.C.K. (Garden of Eden Creation Kit): Terraforming device included in some Vaults to help rebuild the surface.
- V.A.T.S. (Vault-Tec Assisted Targeting System): Combat targeting system integrated with Pip-Boy.
- C.A.M.P. (Construction and Assembly Mobile Platform): Portable building system from Vault 76.
- ZAX: Series of advanced supercomputers (used by Vault-Tec, the Enclave, and the military). ZAX 1.0, ZAX 1.2, the Calculator, John Henry Eden (a ZAX-derived AI president).
- Power Armor: Developed by West Tek for the U.S. military. T-45, T-51b, T-60, and X-01 variants.
- FEV (Forced Evolutionary Virus): Created by West Tek, responsible for Super Mutants, and (debatably) Deathclaws.
- Stealth Boy: RobCo personal cloaking device using refracted light.
- Liberty Prime: Giant anti-communist robot built by the U.S. military with help from RobCo.

### POST-WAR FACTIONS
- Brotherhood of Steel: Military order preserving pre-War technology. Founded by Roger Maxson.
- The Enclave: Remnant of the pre-War U.S. government and military-industrial complex.
- New California Republic (NCR): Democratic nation-state rising from Vault dweller descendants in California.
- Caesar's Legion: Slavers modeled after the Roman Empire, led by Edward Sallow.
- The Institute: MIT-descended organization creating synths (synthetic humans) in the Commonwealth.
- Railroad: Underground movement dedicated to freeing synths.
- Minutemen: Commonwealth militia defending settlements.
- Mr. House / Robert House: Pre-War tech magnate who preserved himself and controls New Vegas.

### THE FALLOUT WORLD
The Fallout universe diverged from our timeline after WWII. Transistors were never miniaturized -- instead, vacuum tube technology was perfected, leading to a retro-futuristic 1950s aesthetic that persisted into the 2070s. Nuclear fusion powered cars (Corvega, Chryslus Highwayman), household robots (Mr. Handy), and the entire American way of life. The Resource Wars (2052-2077) saw nations fighting over dwindling petroleum reserves. The Sino-American War (2066-2077) was fought in Alaska and mainland China. On October 23, 2077, it all ended in nuclear fire.

The post-War wasteland is populated by: humans, ghouls (radiation-scarred humans, some feral), Super Mutants (FEV-mutated), Deathclaws (engineered weapons), Radscorpions, Mirelurks, Mole Rats, Bloatflies, Cazadores, Yao Guai, and countless other irradiated horrors.

Currency: Bottle caps (backed by the water merchants of the Hub; later by water purification in some regions). Pre-War money is mostly worthless.
